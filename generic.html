Is Messi truly a superstar?

The Major League Soccer (MLS), much like other prominent sports leagues in the United States (NBA, NFL, etc.), operates within a distinctive business framework, where clubs serve as franchises under a private company.This organizational structure, coupled with its specific corporate guidelines, significantly influences team management and the construction of player rosters. The recent inclusion of soccer superstars like Lionel Messi has marked a pivotal moment for the league, prompting questions about how these stars impact team performance and the overall popularity of the league. Additionally, it raises the question of what insights corporate organizations can glean from this phenomenon.

As I delved into the exploration of this intriguing intersection between sports and business, I found a wealth of information available for qualitative analysis. However, when it came to obtaining quantitative data on Messi's performance in the MLS, the landscape was less populated. Notably, the MLS official page offered remarkable interactive tables that allowed ranking teams and players based on various metrics such as Games Played, Goals Scored, Penalty Kicks taken, and Penalty Conceded. This discovery led me to the world of Web Scraping, a technique designed for extracting data from websites.

In my initial attempts, I utilized Beautiful Soup, a Python library tailored for web scraping HTML and XML files. While it provided a parse tree for easy navigation and data extraction, I encountered challenges such as incorrect data generation and missing information. Upon further investigation, I learned that interactive tables, like the ones I sought to extract, are often dynamically populated using JavaScript, requiring a different approach. After considering various alternatives, I opted for Selenium, a tool that automates interactions with websites, proving particularly effective for scraping dynamic or interactive content.

Executing this decision involved additional steps, including downloading the appropriate version of "ChromeService" for my computer to enable execution in my Chrome browser. Once accomplished, I inspected the webpage, identified the relevant elements, used "CSS_selector" to determine the necessary columns and rows, and ultimately downloaded the information into a CSV file. This meticulous process aimed to extract data about each player on the Inter Miami team and every football team in the MLS. During the inspection of these tables, subtle differences in their composition emerged. The most noteworthy of these is a comparison between Messi's performance and the weighted mean of other players.
